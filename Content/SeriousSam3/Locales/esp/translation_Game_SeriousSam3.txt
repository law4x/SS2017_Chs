Adjust the delay until the rendered controller perfectly matches the controller in the video feed.
Menu.MixedReality.Help.ImposterAdjustment=Player imposter is a rectangle rendered in the scene that contains a part of chroma keyed video feed which is intended to represent the player inside the game.\nSince the lighting of the player in the studio generally doesn't match the lighting in the game level, we often need to ajust the player imposter brightness in order to lighten or darken the player.\n\nIn this step you can also adjust the part of the video feed that is used for player imposter, by adjusting the imposter height and width. Since player imposter is always centered around the center of the player (a point between the headset and the floor), by limiting the imposter height and width, we can cut away the part of the scene that is not near the player. This allows us to have a studio not entirely covered by the green screen - green screen just needs to cover the player.
